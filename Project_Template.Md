1. What is the problem you are solving?
> Businesses or your audience care about problems. Make the problem clear and relatable

Fuel prices in Kenya are a major economic and political issue because they directly affect transport costs, food prices, electricity, and the overall cost of living. Whenever fuel prices rise, political leaders and media discussions often point to wars and geopolitical tensions in regions such as the Middle East or Eastern Europe as the main cause. However, it is not always clear whether these increases are truly caused by global conflicts alone or whether other factors such as taxes, exchange rates, global market speculation, and local pricing structures also play a major role.

This project investigates whether major geopolitical conflicts actually correlate with increases in global oil prices and whether those increases are reflected in Kenyan fuel prices regulated by EPRA. It also explores whether the public narrative around war and fuel prices fully explains what consumers experience locally.

> Don’t just say “dashboard done.”

The project aims to determine whether spikes in geopolitical conflict actually align with increases in global oil prices or whether the relationship is sometimes overstated in public discussion. Another important insight is whether all conflicts affect oil prices equally, or whether conflicts involving strategic oil producing regions and shipping routes have a stronger impact.

The project also investigates how global oil price changes are reflected in Kenyan fuel prices and whether taxes, regulation, and delayed EPRA adjustments contribute more significantly to the final consumer price than global conflict alone. By comparing global oil prices with Kenyan petrol and diesel prices over time, the analysis can help separate the impact of war related market shocks from local pricing factors.

The goal is not only to show trends, but also to challenge assumptions and provide a clearer, evidence based understanding of why fuel prices rise.




4. How would a business or a community (for Social Impact Projects) benefit from your work?
> Be specific. Think money saved, process improves, better decisions made
> That’s your **real value**

This project can help businesses in transport, logistics, manufacturing  and agriculture better understand the factors driving fuel price changes so they can plan budgets and operational costs more effectively. By identifying whether fuel price increases are linked to global market shocks, taxes, or local policy adjustments, businesses can make more informed financial decisions during periods of instability.

For the public, the project improves transparency around fuel pricing in Kenya by helping people understand whether global conflicts are the main reason behind price increases or whether local economic and regulatory factors also play a major role. This can contribute to more informed public discussion around fuel policy, taxation, and cost of living issues.
